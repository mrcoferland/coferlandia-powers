# Opportunity Report

## 1. Executive Summary
DeFi risk alert dashboard using public data. Educational example only; no income is guaranteed.

## 2. Opportunity
A non-trading dashboard that monitors public DeFi risk signals such as TVL drops, stablecoin depegs, hack disclosures, protocol revenue changes, and bridge incidents.

## 3. Why Now
DeFi users and analysts face fragmented risk information, but direct trading automation is unsafe and out of scope.

## 4. Evidence
Facts to verify: public docs, changelogs, app reviews, community complaints, and comparable products. This example intentionally avoids fake source claims.

## 5. Source Quality
Use `source-quality-scoring` before acting. Prefer primary documentation and fresh community evidence over promotional blog posts.

## 6. Target Customer
Crypto researchers, DAO operators, risk teams, newsletters, and individual analysts who want alerts, not trades.

## 7. Business Model
Paid dashboard, newsletter, API, or productized research service.

## 8. Technical MVP
Pull public APIs, store normalized events, compute risk flags, and send human-reviewed alerts.

## 9. Unit Economics
Use ranges. Expected early costs: hosting, data/API calls, domain, and Diego's time. Validate willingness to pay before scaling.

## 10. Risks
Data quality, false positives, regulatory interpretation, hype cycles, and accidental investment-advice framing.

## 11. 48-Hour Experiment
Create a one-page demo, collect 5 target-user conversations, and test one core automated output.

## 12. Kill Criteria
Kill if target users do not recognize the pain, data is unavailable, legal/platform risk is unacceptable, or no one commits to a concrete next step.
