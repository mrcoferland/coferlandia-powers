# Opportunity Report

## 1. Executive Summary
Public API pricing change monitor for content and affiliate opportunities. Educational example only; no income is guaranteed.

## 2. Opportunity
A monitor that tracks public SaaS/API pricing pages and changelogs, detects changes, and creates review prompts for content, comparison pages, or affiliate assets.

## 3. Why Now
AI and developer-tool pricing changes frequently, creating demand for timely comparisons and migration guidance.

## 4. Evidence
Facts to verify: public docs, changelogs, app reviews, community complaints, and comparable products. This example intentionally avoids fake source claims.

## 5. Source Quality
Use `source-quality-scoring` before acting. Prefer primary documentation and fresh community evidence over promotional blog posts.

## 6. Target Customer
Developers, startups, agencies, affiliate publishers, and tool-selection consultants.

## 7. Business Model
Content site, newsletter sponsorships, affiliate revenue, API access, or productized research.

## 8. Technical MVP
URL list, polite fetcher, diff engine, change classifier, markdown report exporter, and weekly digest.

## 9. Unit Economics
Use ranges. Expected early costs: hosting, data/API calls, domain, and Diego's time. Validate willingness to pay before scaling.

## 10. Risks
Scraping terms, SEO competition, affiliate dependence, stale data, and low conversion risk.

## 11. 48-Hour Experiment
Create a one-page demo, collect 5 target-user conversations, and test one core automated output.

## 12. Kill Criteria
Kill if target users do not recognize the pain, data is unavailable, legal/platform risk is unacceptable, or no one commits to a concrete next step.

## 13. Final Score

Scores:
- Revenue potential: 1-5
- Speed to validate: 1-5
- Technical fit with Diego: 1-5
- Automation potential: 1-5
- Data availability: 1-5
- Capital required: 1-5, inverted
- Risk level: 1-5, inverted
- Distribution difficulty: 1-5, inverted

Final score is heuristic, not objective truth.
