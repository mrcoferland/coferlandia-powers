## Note
Adapted for Coferlandia from general internal-comms patterns; wording and examples are original and brand-neutral.

## When to use this skill
Use this skill for:
- progress updates
- project summaries
- incident reports
- FAQ responses
- leadership notes
- team announcements
- status reports

## Writing workflow
1. Identify the audience.
2. Identify the goal of the message.
3. Gather the facts, decisions, dates, and owners.
4. Decide whether the tone should be neutral, urgent, reassuring, or celebratory.
5. Lead with the main point.
6. Separate context, impact, and next steps.
7. End with a clear ask or call to action if one is needed.

## Useful formats
### Status update
- what changed
- what is in progress
- what is blocked
- what comes next

### Incident note
- what happened
- impact
- mitigation
- owner
- next update time

### FAQ
- question
- short answer
- extra detail if needed

## Style rules
- Prefer short paragraphs and bullets.
- Avoid jargon unless the audience uses it daily.
- Do not bury the decision.
- Keep names, dates, and responsibilities explicit.
- If the format is unclear, ask for the intended audience and channel before drafting.
